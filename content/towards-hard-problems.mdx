# Why pick hard problems to solve?

Over the past few months, I've seen many AI startups creating agents. So far, I'd like to say that there are three disctint type so of AI agents I'm seeing. First are creator agents, whcih create software and products to let the end user of the created software and product create other things. Rplit, Cursor, Bolt, etc. These incldue all of the coding assistatns as well. 

The second type are all around sales and marketing. These include contnent generation, automated marketing, sales pipeline, etc. LLMs are amazing at creating things, after all, their most basic use case is to guess the next best word.

And then, third are the others. The AI agents trying to solve hard problems. 

On a side note, I dont' want to put chatbots as agents. There are qutie a few specalized chatbots for specific purposes, but I don't quite think a chatbot qualifies as an agent. 

## other things on my mind

- hard problems are harder to solve and once you do, you're a king
- as I am thinking about alkemy, I am thinking how we can expand the platform to other things. I want to move beyond making chemical cost curves, so am thinking what agentic workflows look like
- I've found that most people who are posing as entrepreneurs on LinkedIn are solving such shallow problems that it is as if they are in it for the money and the fame, not the jounrey and purpose. Fundamentally for me, this is a stupid trajectory of life
- The real challenge is not just to create products that solve hard problems, but to sell them as well. This is where the former influencers have an advantage. Most of the general population is prone to impulse buying, and thus one can earn quick penies from this process. But why do it? 
- for the past 10 months, I've been workingon DevDash Labs. We are a small team tackling problems and experimenting with GenAI. One major thing that I've done wrong is not talking to enought people about their problems and how tech can make it easier to solve these problems 
- but, as we are looking to create our own products, I've found that (1) it is hard to create an app when you've never done it before, (2) it takes time and first mover advantage really matters, and (3) the faster time to launch, the easier it is to make, the smaller your moat
